Teaching Philosophy

It is my goal that each of my students develop a heightened curiosity for the subject and show progress in the following areas:

  • Their knowledge of history
  • Their ability to think analytically
  • Their ability to apply acquired knowledge and critical thinking skills in their endeavors as thoughtful members of their community

Each of these areas builds upon the other, and course structure and assignments should reflect that process.

As the instructor, I guide the student from an understanding of information into analysis and then provide the arena for each individual to develop their own personal critique of the subject. The skills they acquire under my tutelage should promote independent thinking as well as heightened awareness and understanding of the world in which they live, long after they complete coursework.

As an instructor, my enthusiasm, dedication, discipline, and personal love of learning should stand as a model to anyone who enters my classroom.
